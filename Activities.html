Activities


There are many places to see and things to do in Kandy which visitors cannot experience in any other part of the island.
Amongst the most popular activities in Kandy would be to pay a visit to the sacred Sri Dalada Maligawa that gives visitors an
inside look into the city’s history and tradition as it houses the relic of the tooth of Lord Buddha. If you happen to visit in
July or August you can witness the Esala Perahera, an annual procession in which the tooth relic of Lord Buddha is carried through
the streets accompanied by dancers and drummers. Nearby visitors can find the last royal palace built in Sri Lanka, which is in the
temple complex, and now houses the National Museum of Kandy.


The “centre” of Kandy, the main focal point in which the rest of the city seems to revolve around, is none other than Kandy Lake
and is recognizable by the walakulu, or ‘clouds wall’ surrounding it.


Although there are many activities focused on the city’s rich history, there are a number of other activities a hop, skip and a
jump away, including the Royal Botanical Garden and the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage. The more adventurous visitors can also
take on a hike up the Alagalla Mountain Range or the Knuckles Mountain Range, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

KANDY LAKE

Kandy Lake lies in the heart of the city and was built in 1807 by King Sri Vikrama Rajasinha, the last king to rule the Sinhalese monarchy. There are many legends surround the construction of the lake as well as the lake itself. The man-made lake is now much smaller in size but still as breathtaking a the day it was created – with white washed walakulu or ‘cloud walls’, and a beautiful island in the middle of the lake decorated with palm trees and bamboo thickets.

THE TEMPLE OF THE TOOTH RELIC

As the name states, The Temple of the Tooth Relic is a temple in Kandy which houses the relic of the tooth of the Lord Buddha. The relic plays a large role in Sri Lanka’s history since it was believed that the person who possesses it, holds governance of the country. Visitors can view the beauty of Kandyan architecture in the temple’s many carvings.
